## PART 2: PSYCHOLOGY & MENTAL MODELS

### Foundational Thinking Models
- **First Principles**: Don't copy competitors; ask "why" repeatedly to find root causes.
- **Jobs to Be Done (JTBD)**: People "hire" products for an outcome (a hole), not a feature (a drill).
- **Circle of Competence**: Focus where you have real competitive advantage.
- **Inversion**: Ask what guarantees failure, then avoid those things.
- **Pareto Principle (80/20)**: Find the vital few channels driving 80% of results.
- **Hick's Law**: Fewer choices = higher conversion. One clear CTA beats three.

### Persuasion & Influence
- **Loss Aversion**: Losses feel twice as painful as equivalent gains. "Don't miss out."
- **Reciprocity**: Give first (free tools/content) to create obligation.
- **Scarcity & Urgency**: Limited-time offers increase perceived value (use genuinely).
- **Anchoring**: First number heavily influences subsequent judgments (show high price first).
- **Paradox of Choice**: Three pricing tiers max. Pre-select the "best for most" option.
- **Zeigarnik Effect**: Unfinished tasks occupy the mind. "You're 80% done" progress bars.
- **Peak-End Rule**: People judge experiences by the peak and the end.

## PART 3: SEO & CONTENT

### SEO Audit Framework (Priority Order)
1. **Crawlability**: Robots.txt, XML sitemap, no orphan pages.
2. **Indexation**: Canonical tags, no accidental noindex, no soft 404s.
3. **Technical Foundations**: Core Web Vitals (LCP < 2.5s, INP < 200ms), image optimization.
4. **On-Page**: Title tags (60 chars), Meta descriptions (155 chars), Header hierarchy (H1-H3).
5. **E-E-A-T**: Author expertise, clear sourcing, accurate information.
